JetBlue (United States) carries dogs **in the cabin only**: convenient for small dogs, but off the table if yours is too heavy. Here are the exact conditions, the fee and how to book.

## In the cabin

JetBlue accepts a dog in the cabin if its weight, carrier included, does not exceed **9 kg (20 lb)**. The carrier must be soft-sided and fit under the seat in front of you. Expect about **$125 each way (~€115)**.

*Airline's stated conditions:* dog + carrier max 9 kg (20 lb); $125 each way; max 2 pets.

## In the hold: no

JetBlue does not transport dogs in the hold. No hold or cargo option; cabin only.

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact JetBlue** to add your dog to the booking.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (microchip identification, rabies vaccination, pet passport or health certificate, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (hold). Size it with our [IATA crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

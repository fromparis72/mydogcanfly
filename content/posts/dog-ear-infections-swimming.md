In summer, between swims and heat, **ear infections** are common. Moisture that sits in the ear canal creates ideal conditions for infection, especially in dogs with floppy ears.

*This article is for information only and does not replace advice from a veterinarian.*

## Why moisture leads to ear infections

After a swim in the sea, a lake or a pool, **water stays trapped** in the canal, especially if the ears are floppy and the canal poorly ventilated. This warm moisture encourages bacteria and yeast to multiply — hence the infection.

## Prevent after every swim

**Gently dry** the entrance of the ears with a soft cloth or a gauze pad after every swim, without pushing anything into the canal. A suitable **ear cleaner** (prescribed by your veterinarian) can help keep the canal healthy. Check the ears of at-risk breeds more often in summer.

## Spotting an ear infection

The signs: repeated **head shaking**, **scratching** at the ear, **redness**, an unusual **odor**, discharge, an ear that's painful to the touch. An untreated ear infection gets worse and becomes painful: better to **see a vet** at the first signs than to attempt any deep cleaning yourself.

{{< alerte type="verifier" >}}
Never insert a cotton swab or any product deep into the ear. If the ear is red, smelly or painful, or your dog won't stop scratching, **see a veterinarian**.

Paw pads take a beating on a trip: rocks, hot sand, salt, long distances. Simple aftercare following the effort prevents injuries and keeps your dog on its feet.

*This article is for information only and does not replace advice from a veterinarian.*

## Inspect and clean

After a **hike** or an outing to the **beach**, examine each pad and the **space between the toes**: stones, sand, salt, foxtails, cuts or cracks. **Rinse with fresh water** after the sea (salt and sand irritate and dry out the skin), dry, and gently remove any surface debris.

## Moisturize and repair

Dry pads crack. A **repair balm** for paw pads (based on waxes and oils) moisturizes and forms a protective film; apply it after cleaning, ideally in the evening at rest. On harsh or hot terrain, **boots** protect ahead of time.

## When to see a vet

A **superficial cut** just needs cleaning and monitoring. On the other hand, a **deep wound**, **bleeding** that won't stop, **limping** that lasts, a **burned** pad (asphalt, overheated sand) or an **embedded foreign body** warrant a veterinary visit. A paw pad burn is painful and gets infected easily.

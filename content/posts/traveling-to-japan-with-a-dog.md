## Entry requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| 💉 ISO microchip | Implanted BEFORE the first rabies vaccination. |
| 🦠 Two rabies vaccinations | At least two inactivated/recombinant vaccines after the microchip. |
| 🧪 Rabies titer test (serology) | ≥ 0.5 IU/ml, blood drawn on the day of the second vaccination (or later). |
| ⏳ 180-day waiting period | Between the blood draw and arrival (otherwise quarantine on arrival). |
| 📨 Advance notification | Declaration to the authorities (AQS) + inspection at the airport. |

{{< alerte type="check" >}}
This is one of the longest procedures anywhere: start it 7 to 8 months before departure.

You can travel [by plane](/airline-pet-policies/) (compare airlines that accept dogs). Plan the trip with our [dog flight checker](/tools/can-my-dog-fly/) and, if your dog flies in the hold, size the crate with the [IATA crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

## Practical tips

Plan ahead: most refusals come from a missing document or a missed deadline (a vaccination that is too recent, the rabies titer test, a tapeworm treatment outside its time window). Put together a file with all the supporting documents, and keep a copy. Also prepare your dog for the journey — see our advice on [travel anxiety](/dog-travel-anxiety/) and the [dog first aid kit](/dog-first-aid-kit/).

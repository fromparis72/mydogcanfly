New water, new food, the stress of the trip: travel shakes up a dog's digestion. A few simple precautions prevent most short-lived digestive upsets.

*This article is for information only and does not replace advice from a veterinarian.*

## Common causes

While traveling, diarrhea or vomiting often comes from a **change of food** or **water**, from **stress** (new place, the trip itself), from **motion sickness**, or from swallowing something new (scraps, stagnant water). Most cases are mild and short-lived.

## Prevention

Stick with your dog's **usual kibble** (pack the amount you'll need): avoid any abrupt change of diet. Offer **water you know** or bottled water in areas where tap water is questionable. Don't serve a **big meal right before** hitting the road; a light meal a few hours before departure is better.

## Soothing a mild upset

For mild diarrhea with no other signs, a **short fast** (a few hours without food, keeping water available) followed by a gradual return to easily digestible food often helps. Watch the **hydration**. On the other hand, if symptoms **persist, recur, contain blood**, or come with lethargy, it's time to see a vet.

{{< alerte type="attention" >}}
See a veterinarian if vomiting or diarrhea **lasts beyond 24-48 hours**, contains **blood**, or comes with **lethargy, fever or refusal to drink**: the risk of **dehydration** is real, especially for puppies.

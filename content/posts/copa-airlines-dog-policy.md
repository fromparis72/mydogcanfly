Copa Airlines (Panama) is one of the airlines that accepts dogs **both in the cabin and in the hold**, under conditions. Weight, fees, affected breeds and booking: here's everything you need to know before you reserve.

## In the cabin

Copa Airlines accepts a dog in the cabin if its weight, carrier included, does not exceed **10 kg (22 lb)**. The carrier must be soft-sided and fit under the seat in front of you. Expect roughly **€23 to €115 (~$27–132)**.

*Airline conditions:* ≤ 10 kg (22 lb).

## In the hold: via the cargo service

Copa Airlines does not carry dogs as accompanied baggage: hold transport goes through the **cargo/freight service**, priced by quote and with its own dedicated logistics. Hold travel is as manifest cargo only.

## Brachycephalic breeds

Like most airlines, Copa Airlines applies restrictions to **flat-faced breeds** (bulldogs, pugs, boxers, etc.), which are generally **banned from the hold** because of the respiratory risk. If your dog is brachycephalic, favor the cabin when their weight allows it, and confirm the rule before booking.

> ⚠️ **Heat:** **seasonal embargoes** may apply during periods of high temperatures. Check the dates of your trip. Our advice: [heatstroke in dogs](/dog-heatstroke/).

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact Copa Airlines** to add your dog to the reservation.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (microchip ID, rabies vaccination, health certificate or pet passport, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (hold). Size it with our [IATA dog crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

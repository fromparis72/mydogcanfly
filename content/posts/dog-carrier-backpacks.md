A carrier backpack frees your hands and keeps a small dog safe in the city, on a hike, or on public transit. Chosen well, it becomes a reassuring cocoon; chosen poorly, a source of stress and discomfort.

## Who is a carrier backpack for?

**Small dogs** only: most models support up to 7–8 kg (15–18 lb). The dog must be able to sit inside without being squeezed. For medium and large dogs, it's not suitable.

## The criteria that matter

Prioritize **ventilation** (mesh panels on several sides), an **internal tether** clipped to the harness to prevent jumping out, and **carrying comfort** (wide padded straps, a waist belt that distributes the weight). A rigid, padded base keeps the dog from sagging.

## Getting your dog used to it

As with a crate, habituation is everything: leave the backpack open at home, associate it with treats, then take short outings before a real trip. A dog that panics in the backpack should never be forced into it.

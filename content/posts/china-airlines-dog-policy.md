On China Airlines (Taiwan), dogs are **not allowed in the cabin** (except service dogs): they travel in the **hold**, in an approved crate. Here are the conditions, the weight limit and the procedure to get your companion's flight organized.

## In the cabin: no

China Airlines does not allow dogs in the cabin (except service dogs). The cabin is off-limits to pet dogs — service dogs only.

## In the hold

Since the cabin isn't an option, your dog travels in the pressurized, heated hold in an [IATA-compliant crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/), with a combined dog + crate weight of up to **45 kg (99 lb)**.

*Airline conditions:* Hold as checked baggage (AVIH); fee by zone/weight.

## Brachycephalic breeds

Like most airlines, China Airlines applies restrictions to **flat-faced breeds** (bulldogs, pugs, boxers, etc.), which are generally **banned from the hold** because of the respiratory risk. If your dog is brachycephalic, favor the cabin when their weight allows it, and confirm the rule before booking.

> ⚠️ **Heat:** **seasonal embargoes** may apply during periods of high temperatures. Check the dates of your trip. Our advice: [heatstroke in dogs](/dog-heatstroke/).

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact China Airlines** to add your dog to the reservation.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (microchip ID, rabies vaccination, health certificate or pet passport, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (hold). Size it with our [IATA dog crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

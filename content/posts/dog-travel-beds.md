A familiar bed is a **reassuring landmark** that helps a dog settle in an unknown place — hotel, vacation rental, tent or car. When traveling, you're looking for the sweet spot between comfort, bulk and easy cleaning.

## The right travel-friendly format

Go for a **roll-up or foldable** mattress, lightweight, that slips into the trunk or a bag. The size must let your dog **stretch out completely**. A memory-foam model relieves the joints of senior dogs.

## Cleaning and waterproofing

Choose a **removable, washable cover** and, ideally, a **waterproof underside** for damp ground or little accidents. An extra **thin mat** is handy for insulating your dog from cold tile or a questionable floor.

## The scent trick

Don't wash the bed right before departure: its **familiar smell** reassures your dog in a new environment. Bring along a known blanket or toy as well to reinforce that landmark.

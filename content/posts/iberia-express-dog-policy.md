Iberia Express, Iberia's low-cost subsidiary based in Spain, is one of the airlines that accepts dogs **both in the cabin and in the hold**, under conditions. Weight limits, fees, restricted breeds and booking: here is everything you need to know before you reserve.

## In the cabin

Iberia Express accepts a dog in the cabin if its weight, carrier included, does not exceed **8 kg (18 lb)**. The carrier must be soft-sided and fit under the seat in front of you.

*Airline conditions:* dog + carrier max 8 kg (18 lb); brachycephalic breeds in the cabin only; fee not confirmed.

## In the hold

Above the cabin limit, your dog travels in a pressurized, heated hold, inside an [IATA-compliant crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/).

*Airline conditions:* hold (checked baggage); brachycephalic breeds excluded from the hold.

## Brachycephalic breeds

Like most airlines, Iberia Express applies restrictions to **flat-faced breeds** (bulldogs, pugs, boxers, etc.), which are generally **banned from the hold** because of the respiratory risk. If your dog is brachycephalic, choose the cabin whenever its weight allows, and confirm the rule before booking.

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact Iberia Express** to add your dog to the booking.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (identification, rabies vaccination, pet passport or health certificate, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (hold). Size it with our [IATA crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

{{< enbref >}}
- 🎯 Difficulty level: **Very demanding**
- 📝 **Import permit (MPI)** to request ≥ 6 weeks ahead
- 💉 **Microchip** + **rabies vaccination**
- 🧪 **Serological rabies titer test** (FAVN/RFFIT, ≥ 0.5 IU/ml)
- 🏥 **Mandatory quarantine** (minimum 10 days)
- ⏳ Timeline: **6 to 7 months**
{{< /enbref >}}

New Zealand runs one of the **strictest and longest** import procedures in the world: an import permit, a rabies titer test, then mandatory quarantine on arrival. Allow **6 to 7 months** of preparation, and follow the official MPI guide to the letter.

## Entry requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| 📝 Import permit (MPI) | Apply at least 6 weeks in advance. Your country of origin determines the category (France, for example, is category 3). |
| 💉 Microchip + rabies vaccination | Identification and up-to-date vaccination. |
| 🧪 Serological rabies titer test (FAVN/RFFIT) | ≥ 0.5 IU/ml at an approved laboratory. |
| 🏥 Mandatory quarantine | Minimum 10 days at an MPI-approved facility. |

{{< alerte type="attention" >}}
A **long (6-7 months) and expensive** process: MPI permit, rabies titer test, then mandatory **quarantine**. Any timing mistake can derail the whole trip — follow the official MPI guide step by step.
{{< /alerte >}}

## On the ground: protected wilderness

New Zealand fiercely protects its native wildlife: many natural areas are **off-limits to dogs**, and local regulations are strict. Look into which areas allow dogs once the entry process is squared away.

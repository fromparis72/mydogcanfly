Corsair (France) is one of the airlines that accepts dogs **both in the cabin and in the hold**, under conditions. Weight, fees, affected breeds and booking: here's everything you need to know before you reserve.

## In the cabin

Corsair accepts a dog in the cabin if its weight, carrier included, does not exceed **8 kg (18 lb)**. The carrier must be soft-sided and fit under the seat in front of you. Expect roughly **€50 to €100 (~$58–115)**.

*Airline conditions:* Dog under 8 kg (18 lb) including soft carrier, 1 animal per passenger.

## In the hold

Above the cabin limit, your dog travels in the pressurized, heated hold in an [IATA-compliant crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/), with a combined dog + crate weight of up to **50 kg (110 lb)**. Indicative fee: **€50 to €100 (~$58–115)**.

*Airline conditions:* Over 8 kg (18 lb) in the hold, IATA crate, cap around 50 kg (110 lb).

## Brachycephalic breeds

Like most airlines, Corsair applies restrictions to **flat-faced breeds** (bulldogs, pugs, boxers, etc.), which are generally **banned from the hold** because of the respiratory risk. If your dog is brachycephalic, favor the cabin when their weight allows it, and confirm the rule before booking.

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact Corsair** to add your dog to the reservation.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (microchip ID, rabies vaccination, health certificate or pet passport, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (hold). Size it with our [IATA dog crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

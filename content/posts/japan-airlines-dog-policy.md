On Japan Airlines (JAL), Japan's flag carrier flying worldwide from its **Tokyo Haneda and Narita** hubs, dogs are **not allowed in the cabin** (except service dogs): they travel in the **hold**, in an approved crate. Here are the conditions, the weight limit and the procedure to prepare your companion's flight.

## In the cabin: no

Japan Airlines (JAL) does not allow dogs in the cabin (except service dogs). No exceptions for pet dogs, whatever their size.

## In the hold

Since the cabin is off-limits, your dog travels in a pressurized, heated hold, inside an [IATA-compliant travel crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/), with a combined dog + crate weight of up to **32 kg (70 lb)**.

*Airline conditions:* hold transport as checked baggage; fees not published.

## Brachycephalic (snub-nosed) breeds

Like most airlines, Japan Airlines (JAL) restricts **flat-faced breeds** (Bulldogs, Pugs, Boxers, etc.), which are generally **banned from the hold** because of the respiratory risk. If your dog is brachycephalic, opt for the cabin whenever their weight allows it, and confirm the rule before booking.

> ⚠️ **Heat:** **seasonal embargoes** may apply during periods of high temperatures. Check the dates of your trip. Our advice: [heatstroke in dogs](/dog-heatstroke/).

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact Japan Airlines (JAL)** to add your dog to the reservation.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (microchip ID, rabies vaccination, health certificate or pet passport, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft-sided carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (hold). Size it with our [IATA dog crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

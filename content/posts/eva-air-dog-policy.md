On EVA Air (Taiwan), dogs are **not allowed in the cabin** (except service dogs): they travel in the **cargo hold**, in an approved crate. Here are the conditions, the weight limit and the steps to prepare your companion's flight.

## In the cabin: no

EVA Air does not allow dogs in the cabin (except service dogs). Cabin travel is off-limits for pet dogs.

## In the cargo hold

Since the cabin is off-limits, your dog travels in the pressurized, temperature-controlled hold, in an [IATA-compliant crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/), with a combined dog + crate weight of up to **32 kg (70 lb)**.

*Airline conditions:* hold travel as checked baggage; the fee is charged as a multiple of the excess-baggage rate.

## Brachycephalic breeds

Like most airlines, EVA Air restricts **flat-faced breeds** (Bulldogs, Pugs, Boxers, etc.), which are generally **banned from the hold** because of the respiratory risk. If your dog is brachycephalic, favor cabin travel when an airline's weight limit allows it, and confirm the rule before booking.

> ⚠️ **Heat:** **seasonal embargoes** may apply during periods of high temperatures. Check the dates of your trip. Our advice: [heatstroke in dogs](/dog-heatstroke/).

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact EVA Air** to add your dog to the booking.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (microchip, rabies vaccination, pet passport or health certificate, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (hold). Size it with our [IATA dog crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

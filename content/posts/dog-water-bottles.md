Dehydration sets in fast in dogs, especially in summer and while traveling. A portable hydration setup lets you offer water regularly, anywhere, without waste or mess.

## The bottle with a built-in dispenser

It's the most practical accessory on a walk: one squeeze sends water into an integrated tray, and any water your dog doesn't drink **flows back into the reservoir**. Compact, it solves hydration in the city and on the trail alike. Pick a capacity that matches the length of the outing and your dog's size.

## The collapsible bowl as a backup

For longer breaks (a highway rest stop, a café patio), a **collapsible silicone bowl** unfolds in seconds and stores flat. It's also handy for serving a meal on the move.

## How much water, and how often?

A dog drinks on average the equivalent of several dozen milliliters per kilogram of body weight per day (roughly an ounce per pound), and more during exercise or in hot weather. On the road, offer water **little and often** rather than a large amount at once, and always keep a **reserve** in your bag and in the car.

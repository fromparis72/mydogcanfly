- 🎯 Difficulty level: **Demanding**
- 📝 Import permit (MOCCAE)
- 💉 ISO microchip
- 🦠 Rabies vaccination
- 🧪 Rabies titer test (serology)
- ⏱️ Time to allow: **1 to 2 months**
{{< /enbref >}}

## Entry requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| 📝 Import permit (MOCCAE) | Must be obtained before travel; valid for 90 days. |
| 💉 ISO microchip | Identification is mandatory. |
| 🦠 Rabies vaccination | At least 21 days before travel. |
| 🧪 Rabies titer test (serology) | Required for dogs arriving from France; check whether it applies to your country of origin. |

{{< alerte type="check" >}}
Some breeds are banned or restricted. The rabies titer test adds to the lead time.

## How to get there with your dog

You can travel [by plane](/airline-pet-policies/) (compare the airlines that accept dogs). Plan the transport with our [dog flight finder](/tools/can-my-dog-fly/) and, for the hold, size the crate with the [IATA dog crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

## Practical tips

Plan ahead: most refusals come from a missing document or a missed deadline (vaccination given too recently, rabies titer test, tapeworm treatment outside the required window). Put together a file with all your supporting documents, and keep a copy. Also prepare your dog for the journey — see our advice on [travel anxiety](/dog-travel-anxiety/) and the [dog first aid kit](/dog-first-aid-kit/).

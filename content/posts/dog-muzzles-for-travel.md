- 🧺 Choose a **basket muzzle** that lets your dog pant and drink
- 🚆 Often required for **large dogs** on trains and public transit
- 📏 **Measure the muzzle carefully**: neither too tight nor too loose

- 🚫 Never use a closed muzzle that prevents panting

A muzzle is sometimes **mandatory** for large dogs on trains and certain public transit systems — particularly in Europe — and useful at the vet or during care. Chosen well and introduced well, it's not restrictive for the dog at all.

## The right muzzle: the basket

For travel, choose a **basket muzzle** (plastic or silicone): it lets the dog **pant, drink, and take a treat**, which is essential, especially in hot weather. Absolutely avoid soft "tube-style" muzzles that hold the mouth shut: they prevent heat regulation and become dangerous after just a few minutes.

## Getting the size right

Measure the **length** and **circumference** of your dog's snout. The muzzle should neither squeeze nor slip; the dog must be able to open their mouth to pant. An adjustable model with a head strap keeps them from pulling it off.

## Gentle training

Introduce it **gradually** and **positively**: present it with a treat passed through the basket, have your dog wear it for a few seconds, then extend the duration. **Never use it as punishment**: it must stay neutral, associated with good things.

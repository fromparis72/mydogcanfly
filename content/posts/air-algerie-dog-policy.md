Air Algérie (Algeria) is one of the carriers that accepts dogs **both in the cabin and in the hold**, under conditions. Weight limits, fees, affected breeds and booking: here is everything you need to know before you reserve.

## In the cabin

Air Algérie accepts a dog in the cabin if its weight, carrier included, does not exceed **6 kg (13 lb)**. The carrier must be soft-sided and fit under the seat in front of you. Expect to pay around **€50–120 (~$60–140)**.

*Airline conditions:* Small pets under 6 kg (13 lb); container under 115 cm (45 in) total dimensions; about €50–120 (~$60–140) on international routes.

## In the hold

Above the cabin limit, your dog travels in the pressurized, heated hold, in an [IATA-compliant crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/), for a combined dog + crate weight of up to **32 kg (70 lb)**. Indicative fee: **€100–150 (~$115–175)**.

*Airline conditions:* Up to 32 kg (70 lb) in the hold, then as cargo above that.

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact Air Algérie** to add your dog to the booking.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (microchip, rabies vaccination, pet passport or health certificate, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (hold). Size it with our [IATA crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

Under hard braking, an unrestrained dog is thrown forward and can be seriously injured, injure passengers and interfere with driving. A cargo barrier is one of the devices that make car travel safer and more organized.

## Rigid grille, net or barrier?

A **rigid metal grille** is the strongest and most reliable; it installs between the cargo area and the back seat. A **net** is lighter and more universal but withstands impact less well. Choose based on your vehicle and your dog's size.

## Installing it properly

The divider must be **mounted with no play** (solid anchor points, correct tension). A poorly installed grille can give way in a crash. Custom-fit models hug the cargo area better than universal ones.

## A complement, not a complete solution

A divider keeps the dog from getting into the front, but doesn't fully restrain them within the cargo area. For true restraint, combine it with a **strapped-down crate** or a **crash-tested harness** attached to an anchor point. That combination of safety + comfort is the most reliable setup.

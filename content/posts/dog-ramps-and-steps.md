Jumping into the trunk or onto the bed day after day wears down the joints, especially in growing puppies, senior dogs, long-backed breeds (dachshunds, bassets), and large dogs. A ramp or a set of steps prevents these repeated micro-traumas.

## Ramp or steps?

The **ramp** (an inclined plane) suits **higher climbs** (SUV or station wagon trunk) and dogs that can no longer bend their legs; it needs a bit of space. **Steps** suit **lower heights** (bed, couch, smaller car) and store away easily. In every case, the surface must be **non-slip**.

## The safety points

Check the **maximum load** (suited to your dog's weight), the **stability** (non-slip feet, locking mechanism), and the angle: a ramp that's too steep discourages the dog. A foldable, lightweight model is more practical for travel.

## Getting your dog used to it

Many dogs are wary of the ramp at first. Lay it flat, let your dog explore it with treats, then increase the incline gradually. Never force it: the goal is for them to use it with confidence.

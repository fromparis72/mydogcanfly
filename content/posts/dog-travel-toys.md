A long trip is boring and sometimes stressful for a dog. A few well-chosen items help them stay busy, chew to release tension, and associate travel with something positive.

## Chewing to relax

Chewing is **calming**: a solid chew toy (tough rubber) or a suitable chew bone helps a dog manage the tension of the trip. Choose a size suited to their jaw and keep an eye on wear.

## Toys that keep them busy for a long time

A **stuffed Kong** (wet food, frozen treats) occupies a dog for long minutes. At rest breaks, a **snuffle mat** or a few hidden treats stimulate the nose and burn mental energy — a mentally tired dog rests better afterward.

## Safety and discretion

On the train or plane, prefer **quiet** toys (no squeaker) so you don't disturb others. Avoid toys with small detachable parts when unsupervised, and remove any dangerous object if the dog is left alone for a moment.

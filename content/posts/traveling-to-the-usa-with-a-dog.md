- 🎯 Difficulty level: **Easy**
- 💉 Readable microchip
- 🗓️ Minimum age 6 months
- 📝 CDC Dog Import Form
- 🩺 Apparent good health
- ✅ **No rabies titer test required**
- ⏱️ Lead time: **A few days (online form)**
{{< /enbref >}}

*These rules apply to dogs arriving from a rabies-free or low-risk rabies country (France, for example, is low-risk).*

## Entry requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| 💉 Readable microchip | Detectable by a universal scanner. |
| 🗓️ Minimum age 6 months | On entry into the United States. |
| 📝 CDC Dog Import Form | Online form; the receipt is valid for 6 months and must be shown on arrival. |
| 🩺 Apparent good health | On arrival. |

{{< alerte type="verifier" >}}
Since August 2024, the CDC has simplified the rules for low-risk countries such as France: no rabies certificate and no quarantine.
{{< /alerte >}}

## How to get there with your dog

You can travel [by plane](/airline-pet-policies/) (compare the airlines that accept dogs). Prepare the trip with our [can-my-dog-fly flight checker](/tools/can-my-dog-fly/) and, for the hold, size the crate with the [IATA dog crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

## Practical tips

Plan ahead: most refusals come from a missing document or a missed deadline (a vaccine given too recently, a rabies titer test, a tapeworm treatment outside the required window). Put together a file with all your supporting documents, and keep a copy. Also prepare your dog for the journey — see our advice on [travel anxiety](/dog-travel-anxiety/) and the [dog first aid kit](/dog-first-aid-kit/).

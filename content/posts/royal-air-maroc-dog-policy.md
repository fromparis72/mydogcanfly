Royal Air Maroc (Morocco) is one of the airlines that accept dogs **both in the cabin and in the hold**, under conditions. Weight limits, fees, affected breeds and booking: here is everything you need to know before you reserve.

## In the cabin

Royal Air Maroc accepts a dog in the cabin if its weight, carrier included, does not exceed **8 kg (18 lb)**. The carrier must be soft-sided and fit under the seat in front of you. Expect about **€70–120 (~$80–140)**.

*Airline conditions:* up to 8 kg (18 lb) container included, 55 × 40 × 20 cm (21.5 × 15.5 × 8 in); about €70 (~$80) for Europe/North Africa.

## In the hold

Above the cabin limit, your dog travels in a pressurized, heated hold, inside an [IATA-compliant crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/), for a combined dog + crate weight of up to **70 kg (154 lb)**. Indicative fee: **€60 (~$70)**.

*Airline conditions:* max 70 kg (154 lb) animal + crate, then cargo; from about €60 (~$70) Europe–North Africa.

## Brachycephalic breeds

Like most airlines, Royal Air Maroc restricts **flat-faced breeds** (bulldogs, pugs, boxers, etc.), which are generally **banned from the hold** because of the respiratory risk. If your dog is brachycephalic, favor the cabin when its weight allows, and confirm the rule before booking.

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact Royal Air Maroc** to add your dog to the booking.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (microchip identification, rabies vaccination, passport or health certificate, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (hold). Size it with our [IATA dog crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

Aegean Airlines (Greece) is one of the carriers that accepts dogs **both in the cabin and in the hold**, under conditions. Weight limits, fees, affected breeds and booking: here is everything you need to know before you reserve.

## In the cabin

Aegean Airlines accepts a dog in the cabin if its weight, carrier included, does not exceed **8 kg (18 lb)**. The carrier must be soft-sided and fit under the seat in front of you. Expect to pay around **€35–65 (~$40–75)**.

*Airline conditions:* 8 kg (18 lb) including carrier; container max 55 × 40 × 23 cm (21.5 × 16 × 9 in).

## In the hold

Above the cabin limit, your dog travels in the pressurized, heated hold, in an [IATA-compliant crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/), for a combined dog + crate weight of up to **32 kg (70 lb)**. Indicative fee: **€90–190 (~$105–220)**.

*Airline conditions:* No hold transport if the connection exceeds 3 hours.

## Brachycephalic (snub-nosed) breeds

Like most airlines, Aegean Airlines restricts **flat-faced breeds** (bulldogs, pugs, boxers, etc.), which are generally **banned from the hold** because of the respiratory risk. If your dog is brachycephalic, opt for the cabin whenever its weight allows, and confirm the rule before booking.

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact Aegean Airlines** to add your dog to the booking.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (microchip, rabies vaccination, pet passport or health certificate, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (hold). Size it with our [IATA crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

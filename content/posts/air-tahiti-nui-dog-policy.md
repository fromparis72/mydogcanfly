Air Tahiti Nui (French Polynesia) carries dogs **in the cabin only**: convenient for small dogs, but not an option if yours is too heavy. Here are the exact conditions, the fees and the steps to follow.

## In the cabin

Air Tahiti Nui accepts a dog in the cabin if its weight, carrier included, does not exceed **8 kg (18 lb)**. The carrier must be soft-sided and fit under the seat in front of you. Expect to pay around **€117–201 (~$135–230)**.

*Airline conditions:* 8 kg (18 lb) or less; long-haul flights.

## In the hold: via the cargo service only

Air Tahiti Nui does not carry dogs as accompanied baggage: hold transport goes through the **cargo/freight service**, priced by quote and with dedicated logistics.

## Brachycephalic (snub-nosed) breeds

Like most airlines, Air Tahiti Nui restricts **flat-faced breeds** (bulldogs, pugs, boxers, etc.), which are generally **banned from the hold** because of the respiratory risk. If your dog is brachycephalic, opt for the cabin whenever its weight allows, and confirm the rule before booking.

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact Air Tahiti Nui** to add your dog to the booking.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (microchip, rabies vaccination, pet passport or health certificate, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (cargo). Size it with our [IATA crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

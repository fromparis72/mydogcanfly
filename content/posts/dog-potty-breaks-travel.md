A long trip or a stay in an unfamiliar place disrupts a dog's bathroom habits. Planning the breaks and keeping cleanup supplies on hand makes the trip calmer for everyone.

*This article is for information only and does not replace advice from a veterinarian.*

## How often to stop

Plan a **potty break every 2 to 4 hours** in the car — more often for a **puppy** (immature bladder control) or a **senior dog**. Make use of rest stops and designated pet-relief areas. Always offer a chance to go **before** getting back on the road and **before** boarding (train, plane, ferry).

## Puppies and anxious dogs: patience

The **stress** of a new environment can either block the urge or trigger an **accident**. Stay **patient** and kind: never scold a dog that relieves itself out of stress — it makes the anxiety worse. Reward your dog when it goes in the right place. A puppy needs very frequent outings, especially after meals and naps.

## Cleanup and good manners

Always keep **waste bags** within reach: it's basic courtesy and often legally required. In the car, a **seat cover** and cleanup supplies (paper towels, enzymatic cleaner) save the day in case of an accident. In a vacation rental, clean up immediately so the lingering odor doesn't invite a repeat.

{{< alerte type="verifier" >}}
A normally house-trained dog that has repeated accidents may be expressing **stress**, a **digestive** issue or a urinary problem: if it persists, ask a veterinarian for advice.

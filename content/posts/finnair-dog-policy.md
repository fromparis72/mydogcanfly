Finnair (Finland) is one of the airlines that accepts dogs **both in the cabin and in the cargo hold**, under conditions. Weight limits, fees, restricted breeds and booking: here's everything you need to know before you reserve.

## In the cabin

Finnair accepts a dog in the cabin if its weight, carrier included, does not exceed **8 kg (18 lb)**. The carrier must be soft-sided and fit under the seat in front of you. Expect roughly **€60 to €130 (~$65 to $140)**.

*Airline conditions:* 8 kg (18 lb) including the carrier; soft carrier max 55 × 40 × 23 cm (21.5 × 15.5 × 9 in).

## In the cargo hold

Above the cabin limit, your dog travels in the pressurized, temperature-controlled hold, in an [IATA-compliant crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/), with a combined dog + crate weight of up to **75 kg (165 lb)**. Indicative fee: **€140 to €650 (~$150 to $710)**.

*Airline conditions:* Cargo only on flights to/via the UK, Hong Kong, the UAE, Australia and South Africa.

## Brachycephalic breeds

Like most airlines, Finnair restricts **flat-faced breeds** (Bulldogs, Pugs, Boxers, etc.), which are generally **banned from the hold** because of the respiratory risk. If your dog is brachycephalic, favor the cabin when its weight allows, and confirm the rule before booking.

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact Finnair** to add your dog to the booking.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (microchip, rabies vaccination, pet passport or health certificate, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (hold). Size it with our [IATA dog crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

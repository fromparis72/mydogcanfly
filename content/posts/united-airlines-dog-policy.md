United Airlines (United States) carries dogs **in the cabin only**: convenient for small dogs, but a non-starter if yours is too heavy. Here are the exact conditions, the fee and the steps to follow.

## In the cabin

United Airlines accepts a dog in the cabin if its weight, carrier included, does not exceed **9 kg (20 lb)**. The carrier must be soft-sided and fit under the seat in front of you. Expect about **$150** per segment.

*Airline conditions:* dog + carrier max 9 kg (20 lb); ~$150 per segment; max 2 pets.

## In the hold: via the cargo service

United Airlines does not carry dogs as accompanied baggage: the hold goes through the **cargo/freight service**, priced by quote and with dedicated logistics. There is no longer a general checked-pet hold option; the PetSafe program is limited, and 22 breeds are banned.

## Brachycephalic breeds

Like most airlines, United Airlines restricts **flat-faced breeds** (bulldogs, pugs, boxers, etc.), which are generally **banned from the hold** because of the respiratory risk. If your dog is brachycephalic, favor the cabin when its weight allows, and confirm the rule before booking.

> ⚠️ **Heat:** **seasonal embargoes** may apply during periods of high temperatures. Check your travel dates. Our advice: [heatstroke in dogs](/dog-heatstroke/).

## How to book

Pet spots on board are limited: **book as early as possible**.

1. Buy your ticket, then **contact United Airlines** to add your dog to the booking.
2. Prepare the **health documents** (microchip ID, valid rabies vaccination, pet passport or health certificate depending on your departure country, sometimes a rabies titer test) — see our guide to [international travel with a dog](/international-travel-with-a-dog/).
3. Choose the right container: an approved soft carrier (cabin) or an [IATA crate](/airline-approved-dog-crate/) (hold). Size it with our [IATA dog crate calculator](/tools/iata-dog-crate-calculator/).

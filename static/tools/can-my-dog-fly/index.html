Flying with your dog: how does it work?

Flying with your dog takes a little preparation, but it's entirely doable on most airlines. This comparison tool helps you find and choose the airline best suited to your trip, based on your dog's weight, your route and the transport conditions in the cabin or the hold. The goal: travel with peace of mind, on the right airline, with no bad surprises on boarding day.

Each airline applies its own rules for transporting dogs: maximum weight accepted in the cabin, fees by zone, whether or not snub-nosed breeds are accepted, heat-related embargoes… The same trip can cost three times as much depending on the airline you choose. Comparing before you book is the best way to travel with your dog in good conditions.

How do you choose the right airline for your dog?

To choose the ideal airline and travel worry-free with your dog, a few criteria make all the difference:

  • Your dog's weight (dog + carrier): below roughly 8 kg (18 lb), the cabin is often possible; above that, the dog travels in the hold.
  • Cabin or hold: not all airlines offer both. Some refuse hold transport, others only offer cargo.
  • The breed: brachycephalic dogs (bulldog, pug, boxer…) are often banned from the hold for health reasons.
  • The route: favor a direct flight to reduce your dog's stress and avoid connections.
  • The price: fees range from €30 (~$35) to more than €700 (~$800) depending on the airline, zone and transport mode.
  • The season: in summer, heat embargoes can suspend hold transport.

Cabin or hold: which option for your dog?

In the cabin, your dog travels at your feet in a soft carrier, under the seat in front of you. It's the most reassuring option, reserved for small dogs (generally up to 8 kg / 18 lb, carrier included). In the hold, the dog travels in a hard-sided IATA-approved crate, in a pressurized, heated compartment: that's the solution for larger dogs. Some airlines no longer accept pets in the passenger hold and refer you to cargo, with a dedicated quote.

How much does flying with a dog cost?

The price of flying with a dog depends on the airline, the zone and the transport mode. Expect roughly €30–70 (~$35–80) for a small dog in the cabin on a European flight, and €100 (~$115) to more than €700 (~$800) for a dog in the hold on a long-haul flight. Frequently asked questions about flying with a dog

Which airlines accept dogs in the cabin?

Most major airlines accept a small dog in the cabin, generally up to 8 kg (18 lb) including the carrier. The comparison tool shows you, for your route, which airlines accept your dog in the cabin or in the hold.

Can my dog travel safely in the hold?

Yes: the hold of commercial aircraft is pressurized and heated. Safety comes down to a properly sized IATA-approved crate and following the airline's rules (brachycephalic breeds, summer heat embargoes).

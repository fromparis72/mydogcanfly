How hot is too hot for a dog?

Dogs don't cool down the way we do. They sweat only through their paw pads and rely mostly on panting, so heat builds up fast — especially in flat-faced (brachycephalic) breeds, large dogs, puppies, seniors, and overweight or thick-coated dogs. This tool reads the current temperature where you are and turns it into three plain answers: is it safe to walk, is the pavement safe for their paws, and would an airline fly your dog in the hold today.

Walking your dog in the heat

As a rule of thumb, caution starts around 77–85 °F (25–29 °C) and walks become risky above roughly 90 °F (32 °C). Humidity matters too: the more humid it is, the less effective panting becomes. On hot days, walk early in the morning or late in the evening, keep it short, stay on grass and in the shade, and carry water. Learn to spot the warning signs in our guide to dog heatstroke.

The pavement test: protect your dog's paws

In full sun, asphalt runs far hotter than the air — about 125 °F (52 °C) when the air is only 77 °F (25 °C), and 143 °F (62 °C) at 87 °F (31 °C). A dog's paw pads can burn above about 125 °F (52 °C). The simplest check is the 5-second test: press the back of your hand to the ground for five seconds — if you can't hold it there, it's too hot for their paws. Flying with a dog in summer: heat embargoes

Heat is also the number-one reason a dog is turned away at the airport. Most airlines suspend transport in the cargo hold when the temperature at departure, connection or arrival is above about 85 °F (29.4 °C), and several apply blanket summer embargoes on hold and cargo travel. Small dogs flying in the cabin are usually unaffected. To see which of 62 airlines accept your dog — and which apply heat embargoes — use our dog flight checker.

Frequently asked questions

At what temperature is it too hot to walk a dog?

Caution starts around 77–85 °F (25–29 °C), and above roughly 90 °F (32 °C) walks become risky — especially for flat-faced, large, senior or overweight dogs. Humidity makes it worse. Walk early or late, keep it short, and carry water.

How hot is pavement compared to the air?

In full sun, asphalt is far hotter than the air: about 125 °F (52 °C) when the air is 77 °F (25 °C), and 143 °F (62 °C) at 87 °F (31 °C). Paw pads can burn above about 125 °F (52 °C). Use the 5-second hand test.

What temperature is too hot to fly a dog in the cargo hold?

Most airlines suspend hold and cargo pet transport above about 85 °F (29.4 °C) at any point of the trip, and some apply seasonal summer embargoes. Cabin travel for small dogs is usually still possible — check each airline with our flight checker.
